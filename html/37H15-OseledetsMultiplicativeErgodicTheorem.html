Oseledets multiplicative ergodic theorem


Oseledets multiplicative ergodic theorem, or Oseledets decomposition, considerably extends the results of Furstenberg-Kesten theorem, under the same conditions.

Consider μ a probability measure, and f:M→M a measure preserving dynamical system. Consider A:M→G⁢L⁢(d,𝐑), a measurable transformation, where GL(d,R) is the space of invertible square matrices of size d. Consider the multiplicative cocycle (ϕn⁢(x))n defined by the transformation A, and assume log+⁡||A|| and log+⁡||A-1|| are integrable.

Then, μ almost everywhere x∈M, one can find a natural number k=k⁢(x) and real numbers λ1⁢(x)>⋯>λk⁢(x) and a filtration

𝐑d=Vx1>⋯>Vxk>Vxk+1={0}

such that, for μ almost everywhere and for all i∈{1,…,k}

  1. 1.

    k⁢(f⁢(x))=k⁢(x) and λi⁢(f⁢(x))=λi⁢(x) and A⁢(x)⋅Vxi=Vf⁢(x)i;

  2. 2.

    limn⁡1n⁢log⁡||ϕn⁢(x)⁢v||=λi⁢(x) for all v∈Vxi\Vxi+1;

  3. 3.

    limn⁡1n⁢log⁡|det⁡ϕn⁢(x)|=∑i=1kdi⁢(x)⁢λi⁢(x) where di⁢(x)=dim⁡Vxi-dim⁡Vxi+1

Furthermore, the numbers ki⁢(x) and the subspaces Vxi depend measurably on the point x.

The numbers λi⁢(x) are called Lyapunov exponents of A relatively to f at the point x. Each number di⁢(x) is called the multiplicity of the Lyapunov exponent λi⁢(x). We also have that λ1=λmax and λk=λmin, where λm⁢a⁢x and λmin are as given by Furstenberg-Kesten theorem.
