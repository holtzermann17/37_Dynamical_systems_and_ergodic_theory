hyperbolic isomorphism


Let X be a Banach space and T:X→X a continuous linear isomorphism. We say that T is an hyperbolic isomorphism if its spectrum is disjoint with the unit circle, i.e. σ⁢(T)∩{z∈ℂ:|z|=1}=∅.

If this is the case, by the spectral theorem there is a splitting of X into two invariant subspaces, X=Es⊕Eu (and therefore, a corresponding splitting of T into two operators Ts:Es→Es and Tu:Eu→Eu, i.e. T=Ts⊕Tu), such that σ⁢(Ts)=σ⁢(T)∩{z:|z|<1} and σ⁢(Tu)=σ⁢(T)∩{z:|z|>1}. Also, for any λ greater than the spectral radius of both Ts and Tu-1 there exists an equivalent (box-type) norm ∥⋅∥1 such that

∥Ts∥1⁢<λ⁢ and ∥⁢Tu-1∥1<λ

and

∥x∥1=max⁡{∥xu∥1,∥xs∥1}.

In particular, λ can be chosen smaller than 1, so that Ts and Tu-1 are contractions.
